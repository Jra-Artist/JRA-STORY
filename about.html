Once upon a time there was a monkey tribe that lived in peace and harmony.
The Monkey King was the protector of all living things using his power and magic stick.

One day, the king suffered from a disease that had no cure.
The Monkey King slowly weakened; he couldn’t fight anymore.

The Monkey King's wife took care of her husband due to his illness.
The world of all living things had lost their powerful protector.
The Monkey King died... (Who's going to protect us?)

Because of the death of the Monkey King,
the group of bad animals took advantage and began to conquer the world.

Every village was destroyed—even the monkey tribe’s home.
The wife had no choice but to hide their son in the lake to save him.
She sacrificed herself to protect him. Sadly, the Monkey King's wife was killed.

While the baby was swept away by the river, he was found by a female wolf who was cleaning herself.

She held the baby and asked her husband, the Wolf King, to take care of the child,
thinking he had been abandoned by his parents.

The Wolf King was hesitant but agreed after hearing his wife’s compassion.
They named the child Adan.

Years passed, and the wolves had a son named Lycon. Both Adan and Lycon grew up together.
But Lycon’s jealousy grew because of how his parents treated Adan like family.

A rivalry developed between Lycon and Adan—fueled by jealousy and competition for affection.

While Adan was providing food to the wolf village, everyone was thankful for his kind heart.

Lycon saw Adan and became annoyed by his popularity and kindness.

Lycon accused Adan of planning to betray the pack.
Adan denied it and expressed his gratitude, saying he only wanted to repay their kindness.

Their argument grew louder, but Adan kept trying to reason with Lycon.

The fight between two species began. Adan continued to persuade Lycon, reminding him that family isn’t defined by species.

After the fight, Adan told Lycon he only wanted to treat his parents and Lycon as his true family.

The Wolf King was shot by a bandit’s arrow while protecting the village. He lost a lot of blood from the wound.

The pack rushed him to the doctor, who said a blood transfusion was needed.
Adan volunteered immediately, his magic monkey blood saving the Wolf King.

Lycon was surprised by Adan’s bravery. The doctor revealed that Adan carried the Monkey King’s magic blood.

The Wolf King recovered, and Lycon apologized to Adan.
They embraced—realizing that family is built on love, not blood.

Lycon asked Adan to go fishing. Their friendly rivalry became a symbol of mutual respect and new beginnings.

THE END
